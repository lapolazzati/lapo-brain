## ISPRA's Risk Analysis Methodology

### Core Concept

**Risk Analysis** = "The most advanced tool for decision support in the management of contaminated sites"

Quantitatively assesses health risks from pollutant exposure.

### The Conceptual Model (Three Required Elements)

Risk to human health exists ONLY when all three are present and connected:

1. **Contamination Source**
   - Identification and characterization
   - What contaminants exist?
   - At what concentrations?

2. **Migration Pathways**
   - Through environmental matrices (soil, groundwater, air)
   - How does contamination move?
   - What routes does it take?

3. **Exposure Targets/Receptors**
   - On or near the site
   - Who/what is exposed?
   - How are they exposed?

**No complete pathway = No risk** (even if contamination exists)

### Methodological Basis

**Standard**: ASTM RBCA (Risk Based Corrective Action)
- E 1739-95
- 2081-00
- PS-104-98

**Framework**: Four-phase approach per National Academy of Science (NAS, 1983)

### Two Analysis Modes

**1. Forward Analysis (Direct Risk Calculation)**:
- Input: Detected contamination levels
- Output: Estimated risk to human health
- Use: "Is this site dangerous?"

**2. Backward Analysis (Remediation Target Calculation)**:
- Input: Acceptable risk criteria
- Output: Site-specific remediation goals (CSR - Risk Threshold Concentrations)
- Use: "How clean does it need to be?"

## CSC vs CSR: Critical Distinction

### CSC - Concentrazione Soglia di Contaminazione (Threshold Concentration)

**What it is**: Generic screening values set by law (D.Lgs. 152/2006, Annex 5)

**Purpose**:
- First-tier screening
- If contamination > CSC → site requires further investigation
- If contamination < CSC → site presumed acceptable

**Characteristics**:
- Same values used nationwide
- Conservative (protective)
- Fast decision-making tool
- No site-specific analysis required

**Example CSC values** (from D.Lgs. 152/2006):
- Benzene (groundwater): 1 μg/L
- Trichloroethylene (groundwater): 1.5 μg/L
- Lead (soil, residential): 100 mg/kg
- Chromium VI (soil, residential): 2 mg/kg

### CSR - Concentrazione Soglia di Rischio (Risk Threshold Concentration)

**What it is**: Site-specific target values calculated through risk analysis

**Purpose**:
- Second-tier, detailed assessment
- Calculated using actual site conditions
- Determines remediation goals
- May be more or less stringent than CSC

**Characteristics**:
- Unique to each site
- Requires conceptual model
- Accounts for actual exposure pathways
- More expensive but more accurate

**When to use CSR**:
- When CSC is exceeded but remediation to CSC is impractical/expensive
- When site-specific conditions suggest lower risk than CSC implies
- When determining exact remediation endpoint

## Environmental Characterization Requirements

### What's Required (per D.Lgs. 152/2006, Title V, Part IV, Annex 2)

**1. Environmental Boring Operations**:
- Drill holes to sample soil at various depths
- Install monitoring wells for groundwater

**2. Soil Sampling**:
- Representative samples from contamination zones
- Multiple depths (surface, subsurface, vadose zone)
- Sufficient density for spatial characterization

**3. Groundwater Sampling**:
- Depth-to-water measurement
- Purging procedures
- Representative samples from aquifer

**4. Analytical Testing**:
- Accredited laboratories
- Methods compliant with Italian standards
- QA/QC protocols

**5. Conceptual Site Model (CSM)**:
- Source identification
- Pathway analysis
- Receptor identification
- Exposure scenarios

### Site Characterization Deliverables

**Documentation Required**:
- Boring logs and well construction details
- Laboratory analytical results
- CSM diagrams
- Exposure pathway maps
- Risk calculations (if CSC exceeded)
- Remediation proposal

**ARPA Review Timeline**:
- Typically 60-90 days for initial review
- Iterations possible if deficiencies found
- Critical path item for project schedule

## Risk Analysis Calculation Process

### Phase 1: Hazard Identification
- What contaminants are present?
- What are their toxicological properties?
- Cancer risk vs. non-cancer risk

### Phase 2: Dose-Response Assessment
- What dose causes adverse effects?
- Slope factors for carcinogens
- Reference doses for non-carcinogens

### Phase 3: Exposure Assessment
- Who is exposed? (Receptors)
- How are they exposed? (Pathways)
  - Direct ingestion of soil
  - Inhalation of vapors
  - Dermal contact
  - Ingestion of contaminated groundwater
- How much exposure? (Exposure factors)
  - Body weight
  - Ingestion rate
  - Exposure duration
  - Exposure frequency

### Phase 4: Risk Characterization
- Calculate risk from all pathways
- Cancer risk: Incremental lifetime cancer risk (ILCR)
  - Target: < 1 × 10⁻⁶ to 1 × 10⁻⁴ (1 in 1 million to 1 in 10,000)
- Non-cancer risk: Hazard quotient (HQ)
  - Target: < 1.0

## Key Takeaways (The TL;DR)

1. **D.Lgs. 152/2006 is the law** → ISPRA provides the methodology to implement it

2. **CSC vs CSR distinction is critical**:
   - CSC = generic screening (fast, conservative)
   - CSR = site-specific targets (accurate, requires analysis)

3. **Conceptual Site Model is mandatory**:
   - Source + Pathway + Receptor = Risk
   - No complete pathway = No risk (even with contamination)

4. **RBCA methodology is standard**:
   - Forward analysis: contamination → risk
   - Backward analysis: acceptable risk → cleanup goals

5. **ARPA approval is the critical path**:
   - Every milestone requires ARPA review
   - Speaking ISPRA's language = faster approvals
   - Non-compliance = delays or rejection

6. **Risk calculation has four phases**:
   - Hazard ID → Dose-Response → Exposure → Risk Characterization
   - Cancer risk target: < 1 in 10,000 to 1 in 1,000,000
   - Non-cancer hazard quotient: < 1.0

7. **ISPRA is more than guidelines**:
   - Database (MOSAICO) for precedent cases
   - Software evaluation and standards
   - Direct technical support (criterimetodologici@isprambiente.it)

8. **Remediation technologies are catalogued**:
   - ISCO and bioremediation are ISPRA-approved
   - CEG's Advanced Oxidation fits within ISPRA framework
   - BioBlend is recognized enhanced bioremediation approach
