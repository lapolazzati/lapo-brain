# Bioremediation Glossary

> Compressed reference for quick recall. Add terms as they appear in lessons.

---

## A

**Advanced Oxidation (ISCO)**: In-Situ Chemical Oxidation using activated persulfate (or peroxide/permanganate) to break down contaminants underground. CEG's core technology. The oxidant (persulfate radical, SO₄•⁻) attacks covalent bonds — especially C-Cl bonds on chlorinated solvents and aromatic rings on hydrocarbons.

**ARPA (Agenzia Regionale per la Protezione dell'Ambiente)**: Regional Environmental Protection Agency. In Lombardia, ARPA reviews and approves every stage of contaminated site remediation.

**Aquifer**: Underground layer of water-bearing permeable rock or sediment. Contamination that reaches an aquifer spreads as a plume.

---

## B

**Bioaccumulation**: The buildup of a substance in an organism over time because the organism absorbs it faster than it can eliminate it. Why persistent contaminants matter — they concentrate up the food chain.

**Bioremediation**: Using living organisms (usually bacteria) to break down contaminants. Two flavors:
- **Biostimulation**: Add nutrients (CEG BioBlend) — wake up the bacteria already there.
- **Bioaugmentation**: Add bacteria — import microbes that can handle the contaminant.

**BTEX**: Benzene, Toluene, Ethylbenzene, Xylene. The four volatile aromatic hydrocarbons that dominate petroleum contamination. Benzene is the most toxic and most regulated. All four are ring structures attacked by both ISCO and bioremediation.

**Bond (covalent)**: Two atoms sharing electrons. The energy in that shared pair is what holds the molecule together. The Lego brick. Breaking it at the right spot strips the molecule's toxic properties.

---

## C

**CSC (Concentrazione Soglia di Contaminazione)**: Generic screening threshold set by D.Lgs. 152/2006, Annex 5. Same for all of Italy. If contamination exceeds CSC → site needs investigation. If below CSC → site is presumed clean. *Example: benzene in groundwater CSC = 1 μg/L.*

**CSR (Concentrazione Soglia di Rischio)**: Site-specific cleanup target calculated through ISPRA risk analysis. Can be higher or lower than CSC depending on actual exposure pathways. *This is what you aim for, not CSC, if site conditions justify it.*

**Chlorinated solvent**: Organic compound containing chlorine atoms bonded to carbon. TCE, PCE, carbon tetrachloride. Persistent in groundwater because C-Cl bonds are strong. The main target of ISCO (persulfate breaks C-Cl bonds). More dense than water (DNAPLs — sink to the bottom of aquifers).

**Conceptual Site Model (CSM)**: The three-element framework required by ISPRA: Source (what contaminants, where) + Pathway (how they move) + Receptor (who is exposed). No complete pathway = no risk, even if contamination exists.

---

## D

**D.Lgs. 152/2006**: Italian Environmental Standards Decree. Title V, Part IV covers contaminated sites. The law. Sets CSC values in Annex 5.

**DNAPL (Dense Non-Aqueous Phase Liquid)**: A contaminant liquid heavier than water that sinks through the aquifer to pool on low-permeability layers. Chlorinated solvents are DNAPLs — they go straight down and are hard to find. Petroleum hydrocarbons are LNAPLs (float on water).

---

## E

**Enhanced Bioremediation**: See Bioremediation / Biostimulation.

---

## G

**Groundwater plume**: The spreading zone of contaminated groundwater extending from the source. Moves with groundwater flow. Characterizing the plume (size, direction, concentration gradient) is the core of site characterization.

---

## H

**Half-life**: Time for half of a contaminant to degrade under specific conditions. Not a fixed property — depends on temperature, pH, microbial activity, oxygen levels. *Example: TCE half-life in anaerobic groundwater = years to decades; in an active ISCO treatment zone = hours to days.*

**Hydrocarbon**: Organic compound made of only carbon and hydrogen. Petroleum is mostly hydrocarbons. Generally biodegradable (bacteria eat them) but some are recalcitrant. BTEX are hydrocarbons.

---

## I

**ISCO (In-Situ Chemical Oxidation)**: See Advanced Oxidation.

**ISPRA (Istituto Superiore per la Protezione e la Ricerca Ambientale)**: National institute providing the technical methodology for contaminated site assessment. They write the guidelines ARPA uses.

---

## L

**LNAPL (Light Non-Aqueous Phase Liquid)**: A contaminant liquid lighter than water that floats on the water table. Petroleum hydrocarbons are LNAPLs — they sit on top of groundwater.

---

## M

**MOSAICO**: ISPRA's national database of contaminated sites. 38,556 bonifica procedures tracked. Searchable by region, contaminant, regulatory stage.

---

## O

**Oxidation**: Chemical reaction where a substance loses electrons. In remediation, strong oxidants (persulfate radicals) rip electrons away from contaminant molecules, breaking their bonds. The molecular mechanism behind ISCO.

---

## P

**PAH (Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbon)**: Multiple benzene rings fused together. From incomplete combustion (coal tar, creosote, vehicle exhaust). More rings = more persistent. Some are carcinogenic.

**PCE (Tetrachloroethylene / Perchloroethylene)**: CCl₂=CCl₂. Four chlorines on a two-carbon backbone. Dry cleaning solvent. Denser than water (DNAPL). Very persistent. ISCO target.

**Persulfate**: S₂O₈²⁻. The oxidant in CEG's Advanced Oxidation. When activated (heat, iron, high pH), it forms persulfate radicals (SO₄•⁻) — extremely reactive species that attack and break contaminant bonds. The activated form is what does the work.

**PFAS (Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances)**: "Forever chemicals." C-F bonds are among the strongest in organic chemistry — almost nothing in nature breaks them. Extremely persistent, bioaccumulative. Requires specialized treatment (hybrid bioelectrochemical, not just ISCO). The Veneto Miteni/Trissino case covers 380 km².

**Plume**: See Groundwater plume.

**Piano di Caratterizzazione**: Site characterization plan — first phase of Italian remediation. Drill, sample, analyze, build the conceptual site model.

---

## R

**Radical (free radical)**: A molecule or atom with an unpaired electron. Highly reactive, short-lived. Persulfate radicals (SO₄•⁻) are the active species in ISCO — they steal electrons from contaminant bonds, breaking them.

**Redox (Reduction-Oxidation)**: The chemical environment's electron balance. Low redox (reducing, anaerobic) favors reductive dechlorination (bacteria strip off chlorines). High redox (oxidizing, aerobic) favors oxidation (persulfate attacks bonds). Site redox conditions determine which CEG technology works best.

**Reductive dechlorination**: Bacteria under anaerobic conditions remove chlorine atoms one at a time from chlorinated solvents, replacing them with hydrogen. This is how enhanced bioremediation handles TCE and PCE. Slower than ISCO but uses less energy.

---

## S

**SIN (Sito di Interesse Nazionale)**: Site of National Interest — the most contaminated/complex sites in Italy, managed at national level (MASE) instead of regional (ARPA). Higher scrutiny, larger scale. Check if Milano Piano Casa sites fall under SIN.

---

## T

**TCE (Trichloroethylene)**: CHCl=CCl₂. Three chlorines on a two-carbon backbone. Metal degreasing solvent. Denser than water (DNAPL). CSC in groundwater = 1.5 μg/L. The Lego brick: the C-Cl bonds.

**Threshold**: See CSC and CSR.
