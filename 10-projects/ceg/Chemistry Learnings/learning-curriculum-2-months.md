# CEG Chemistry Learning Curriculum — 2 Months

**Source**: Conversation May 21, 2026
**Context**: ADHD-friendly curriculum for understanding remediation chemistry without textbooks

## Core Concept: The Lego Brick Metaphor

From the geologist: Chris Young's work is fundamentally about **breaking covalent bonds at the right position** so the molecule loses the property that made it toxic/persistent.

The "Lego brick" is usually:
- A chlorine atom
- A benzene ring
- A specific carbon-carbon bond

**Find it, break it → the molecule becomes something biology can finish off.**

---

## Month 1 — Build the Mental Model

### Week 1-2: Chemistry Foundation (without dying of boredom)

**"Napoleon's Buttons: 17 Molecules That Changed History"** — Le Couteur & Burreson
- Each chapter = one molecule + the story of what it did to the world
- You'll learn organic chemistry by accident
- **START HERE** — Perfect ADHD pacing

**"Periodic Tales"** — Hugh Aldersey-Williams
- Same format, element-by-element
- Read chapters on: chlorine, arsenic, lead, mercury (these are your contaminants)

### Week 3-4: How Contamination Actually Works

**"Silent Spring"** — Rachel Carson
- Read not for politics but for chemistry explanation
- She explains bioaccumulation, persistence, and metabolic pathways better than any textbook
- Chapters are short

**"Toms River: A Story of Science and Salvation"** — Dan Fagin (Pulitzer winner)
- Single contaminated site told as detective story
- Chemistry + hydrogeology + epidemiology + regulation
- **Closest thing to a CEG case study in book form**

---

## Month 2 — Get Technical Without Going to Grad School

### Week 5-6: The Remediation Playbook

**"In Situ Chemical Oxidation for Groundwater Remediation"** — Siegrist, Crimi, Simpkin (SERDP/ESTCP series)
- The bible for ISCO
- **Don't read cover-to-cover**
- Read: Chapter 2 (reaction chemistry) + Chapter 5 (persulfate)
- Skim the rest
- **This is exactly what CEG does with Advanced Oxidation**

**"Bioremediation: Applied Microbial Solutions for Real-World Environmental Cleanup"** — Atlas & Philp
- Older but still clearest intro to BioBlend-style biostimulation
- Chapter on hydrocarbon degradation is gold

### Week 7-8: The Italian / European Layer

**"Bonifica dei siti contaminati"** — Beretta, Pellegrini (Hoepli)
- The standard Italian reference
- Heavy but skimmable
- Read sections on:
  - D.Lgs. 152/2006 procedure
  - ARPA workflow
- You don't need the engineering math

**ISPRA Guidelines** (free PDFs from isprambiente.gov.it)
- Linee Guida per l'applicazione dell'Analisi di Rischio
- Free, in Italian, exactly the language ARPA uses
- **Read the worked examples**

## How to Actually Do This With ADHD

### Learning Strategies

**One book at a time, two formats in parallel**:
- Print book + audiobook of something different
- When focus dies on one, switch

**Draw molecules instead of taking notes**:
- Literally sketch the contaminant
- Circle the bond that gets broken
- Three minutes per molecule
- Your spatial memory will outperform your text memory

**Build a contamination flashcard deck** (Anki or paper):
- One card = one contaminant
- **Front**: name + structure
- **Back**:
  - Where it comes from
  - Why it's toxic
  - Which CEG tech handles it
  - Half-life
  - Italian regulatory threshold (CSC)
- After 60 days: ~50 cards
- You'll know more than most Italian commercialisti pretending to do environmental DD

**Apply it the same day**:
- When you read about persulfate chemistry on Monday
- On Tuesday, look at a real CEG project that used it
- The Lego brick metaphor only sticks when you've turned the brick yourself a few times
