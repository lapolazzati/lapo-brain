### 0003 — ISCO Deep Dive: The Chemical Sledgehammer
**Status:** Complete
**Published:** https://present-trellis-fjyy.here.now/chemistry-for-bioremediation/lessons/0003-isco-deep-dive.html
**Structure:**
- What is a radical? Unpaired electron → unstable → reactive
- Persulfate (S₂O₈²⁻) → activation (heat, Fe²⁺, high pH) → 2 × SO₄•⁻
- The electron theft: SO₄•⁻ + C-Cl bond → Cl⁻ + SO₄²⁻ + dechlorinated fragment
- What's left: chloride ion (Cl⁻, harmless), sulfate (SO₄²⁻, natural), CO₂ + H₂O (mineralization)
- Byproducts and their fate — the question ARPA will ask
- Activation methods comparison: heat vs iron vs alkaline
- Interactive: draw the electron transfer for TCE dechlorination

### 0005 — Heavy Metals: Different Beast, Different Rules ✅
**Status:** Complete
**Goal:** Lapo understands why metals need immobilization/reduction, not destruction, and can explain Cr-VI remediation.
**Structure:**
- You can't "destroy" a metal — it's an element, it stays
- The strategy: change oxidation state to reduce toxicity/mobility
- Cr-VI (toxic, mobile, carcinogenic) → Cr-III (low toxicity, precipitates as hydroxide)
- Reduction via ISCO byproducts or biological reduction
- Lead: phosphate stabilization (forms insoluble pyromorphite)
- Cadmium, mercury, arsenic — overview
- CSC thresholds for metals in soil and groundwater
- Interactive: classify each metal by its remediation strategy

### 0006 — PFAS: The Forever Chemical
**Goal:** Lapo can explain why C-F bonds are different and what it takes to break them.
**Structure:**
- The C-F bond: strongest in organic chemistry (~485 kJ/mol vs ~350 for C-Cl)
- Why ISCO can't do it: the bond energy is higher than what persulfate radicals can deliver
- The Veneto case: Miteni/Trissino, 380 km², 300,000 people — why this matters in Italy
- What DOES work: hybrid bioelectrochemical, foam fractionation, electrochemical oxidation
- CEG's positioning: if Milano sites have PFAS, this is a different conversation
- PFOS, PFOA, GenX — the family tree
- Interactive: compare bond energies — why C-F laughs at persulfate

### 0007 — Redox: The Hidden Switch
**Goal:** Lapo can look at dissolved oxygen or ORP data and immediately know which technology to recommend.
**Structure:**
- ORP (oxidation-reduction potential): the master variable
- The redox ladder: O₂ → NO₃⁻ → Mn⁴⁺ → Fe³⁺ → SO₄²⁻ → CO₂
- Aerobic zone: ISCO territory, bacteria love oxygen
- Anaerobic zone: BioBlend territory, reductive dechlorination dominates
- The transition zone — where both can work
- What happens when you inject persulfate into an anaerobic zone (it still works, but differently)
- Reading real ORP data from a site report
- Interactive: given ORP and DO values, choose the technology
