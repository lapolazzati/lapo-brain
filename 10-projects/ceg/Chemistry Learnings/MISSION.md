# Mission: Bioremediation Chemistry for CEG Italia

## Why
I run CEG Italia. We're pursuing the Milano bonifica tender and building a pipeline of Italian remediation projects. I need to discuss bioremediation chemistry with ARPA regulators, technical buyers (industrial site owners), and remediation contractors without sounding uninformed. The concrete outcome is winning the Milano tender and representing CEG's technology credibly in technical conversations — not becoming a chemist, but never being the least-informed person in the room.

## Success looks like
- I can explain CEG's Advanced Oxidation (ISCO) and BioBlend (enhanced bioremediation) to a technical audience, including the reaction chemistry, without hand-waving
- I know the Lego-brick metaphor cold: which covalent bonds get broken on which contaminant classes, and why breaking them strips toxicity
- I can navigate CSC/CSR threshold tables and ISPRA risk analysis framework in conversation with ARPA Lombardia
- A contractor asks me about persulfate activation, redox conditions, or plume behavior — I don't freeze
- I can read a site characterization report and identify which CEG technology matches which contaminant profile

## Constraints
- ADHD-friendly: story-driven, multi-format, no textbooks
- Present-me needs rapid conceptual grounding (tender-adjacent); future-me has a 2-month horizon
- This is operational/strategic knowledge, not graduate-level environmental engineering
- Learning happens in the context of real Milano sites and real CEG technologies — not abstract chemistry

## Out of scope
- Running actual lab analyses or operating field equipment
- Engineering-level remediation design math (reaction kinetics, aquifer modeling)
- Regulatory compliance outside Italy/ARPA context
- Non-remediation chemistry (industrial synthesis, materials science, etc.)
