# Milano Piano Casa Bando Sites - Locations & Industrial History

*Research conducted: May 13, 2026*
*Bando deadline: June 26, 2026*

---

## Summary

The Municipality of Milan opened three bandi (tenders) for the Piano Straordinario per la Casa Accessibile with proposals due by June 26, 2026. This document details the **locations, sizes, previous industrial uses, and contamination** at each site.

---

## Three Bando Sites

### 1. Sant'Elia (Lampugnano) - Ex Palasharp Site

**Location:**
- Via Sant'Elia 33, Milano
- Lampugnano district (northwestern Milan)
- ~100 meters from MM1 Lampugnano metro stop

**Size:**
- 18,000 square meters of publicly-owned land

**Previous Industrial/Commercial Use:**

**NOT traditional heavy industry** - this was a **sports/entertainment venue**:

- **Built:** September 26, 1986 (inaugurated by Frank Sinatra)
- **Purpose:** Temporary sports arena constructed after San Siro Palasport roof collapsed in January 1985
- **Previous names:** Mazda Palace, PalaTrussardi, PalaVobis, PalaSharp
- **Activities:** Basketball games, concerts, major events
- **Closed:** 2012
- **Recent history:** Planned to become Milano Hockey Arena for 2026 Winter Olympics, but project failed due to increased decontamination costs
- **Current status:** Structure has been dismantled

**Contamination Status:**

- **Contaminated:** YES
- **Remediation budget allocated:** Up to €6.2 million for environmental remediation
- **Contamination type:** Not specified in sources, but significant enough to derail Olympic hockey arena project
- **Additional costs:** Up to €3.15 million for demolition of ex-Palasharp structure

**CEG Opportunity:**
- High priority - €6.2M remediation budget
- Site already assessed as contaminated
- Demolition + remediation needed before residential development
- Timeline urgent (June 26 deadline for bando proposals)

### 2. Bovisasca (Bovisa) Site

**Location:**
- Via Bovisasca, Milano
- Bovisa/Bovisasca district (northern Milan)

**Size:**
- 1,800 square meters

**Previous Industrial Use:**

**HEAVY INDUSTRIAL - Chemical manufacturing district**:

- **18th century:** Small farmstead in open countryside
- **1862:** **Giuseppe Candiani** founded establishment for sulfuric acid production
- **1880s:** Candiani transferred operations to Bovisa, between Bovisasca road and railway tracks
- **1897:** Established joint-stock company **Giuseppe Candiani & C.** (managed with sons Attilio and Ettore)
- **Products manufactured:**
  - **Copper arsenite** (arsenic-containing compound) - for dyeing wool and silk
  - Chrome yellow
  - Ferrigine (iron-based pigments)
  - Sulfuric acid
- **19th-20th century transformation:** Rapid industrialization - earned nickname **"piccola Manchester di Milano"** (little Manchester of Milan)
- **Industries:** Heavy industrial and productive district with large working-class population
- **Social transformation:** Rural farmers → urban factory workers within 40 years

**Contamination Status:**

- **Contaminated:** YES - well documented
- **Primary contaminant:** **Arsenic waste from former chemical plant**
- **Severity:** Repeatedly denounced by local committees, ecology organizations, and institutions
- **Remediation budget allocated:** Up to €1.6 million for environmental remediation
- **Additional issues:** Informal Romani settlements developed in area in late 20th century

**Industrial Heritage Context:**
- Part of Milan's broader industrial transformation
- Chemical manufacturing = high contamination risk (similar to Seveso disaster context)
- Arsenic contamination = serious health/environmental hazard requiring specialized remediation

**CEG Opportunity:**
- Medium priority - €1.6M remediation budget (smaller than Sant'Elia)
- **Chemical contamination = CEG specialty** (bioremediation for organic compounds, potential arsenic treatment)
- Arsenic = heavy metal contamination (may require different approach than standard bioremediation)
- Well-documented contamination = clear scope

---

### 3. San Romanello (Quinto Romano) Site

**Location:**
- Via San Romanello, Milano
- Quinto Romano district (northwestern Milan, Municipality 7)

**Size:**
- 7,200 square meters

**Previous Use:**

**NOT traditional heavy industry** - primarily **agricultural/transport infrastructure**:

- **Until 1960s:** Rural district with 8-9 cascine (farms)
- **1960s onward:** Rapid urbanization during Italy's economic boom + southern immigration
- **Recent use:** Area of 27,000 sqm (includes this 7,200 sqm parcel) previously managed by **ATM** (Milan's public transport authority)
- **Considered use:** Municipality evaluated site for recycling center (never built)

**Contamination Status:**

- **Unknown from available sources**
- **No remediation budget allocated** (unlike Sant'Elia €6.2M and Bovisasca €1.6M)
- **Implication:** Either not contaminated, or contamination not yet assessed

**Historical Context:**
- Agricultural → urban transformation (less likely to have industrial contamination)
- ATM transport authority use (unlikely to cause significant contamination unless vehicle maintenance/fueling)
- Quinto Romano = historically rural, not industrial hub like Bovisa

**CEG Opportunity:**
- **Lower priority** - no contamination budget allocated
- May not require environmental remediation
- Could still need soil assessment/testing as part of due diligence
- Opportunity: Offer low-cost feasibility study (€5-15K) to assess if remediation needed

## Bando Terms (All Three Sites)

**Grant structure:**
- Surface rights for **90 years**
- Symbolic transfer price: **€1 per square meter**

**Housing requirements:**
- Build and manage subsidized rental housing
- Permanent rental for entire 90-year duration
- **Maximum rent:** €90/sqm/year (~€375/month for 50 sqm apartment)
- **Minimum subsidized rental period:** 30 years

**Obligations:**
- **Sant'Elia:** Quota of public housing units must be built and transferred to Municipality **free of charge** upon completion

**Timeline:**
- Bando opened: April 27, 2026
- **Proposal deadline:** June 26, 2026, 12:00 PM
- **CEG action window:** ~6 weeks to identify contractor partner + prepare bid

## CEG Strategic Prioritization

### Priority 1: Sant'Elia (Ex Palasharp) - €6.2M Remediation

**Why:**
- Largest remediation budget (€6.2M)
- Confirmed contamination (derailed Olympic project)
- 18,000 sqm = substantial project size
- Demolition + remediation = comprehensive scope

**CEG positioning:**
- Offer hybrid remediation approach (likely mixed contaminants from event venue operations)
- Partner with demolition contractor for integrated bid
- Emphasize cost efficiency vs. excavation/disposal

**Revenue potential:** €1.8M - €2.5M (30-40% of €6.2M remediation budget)

### Priority 2: Bovisasca - €1.6M Remediation

**Why:**
- Confirmed arsenic contamination from chemical plants
- Industrial heritage = likely mixed contamination beyond arsenic
- "Piccola Manchester" history = decades of industrial pollutants

**CEG positioning:**
- **Challenge:** Arsenic = heavy metal (not CEG's core bioremediation strength)
- **Opportunity:** Mixed contamination likely includes organic compounds (CEG specialty)
- May need partner with arsenic remediation capability
- Hybrid approach: CEG handles organic compounds, partner handles heavy metals

**Revenue potential:** €0.5M - €0.6M (30-40% of €1.6M budget)

**Risk:** Arsenic remediation may require specialized technology outside CEG's current portfolio

### Priority 3: San Romanello - No Allocated Budget

**Why lower priority:**
- No contamination budget allocated
- Agricultural/transport history = lower contamination probability
- Unknown assessment status

**CEG positioning:**
- Offer **feasibility study** (€5-15K) to assess contamination
- If contamination found: pivot to remediation proposal
- If clean: exit gracefully, focus on Sant'Elia/Bovisasca

**Revenue potential:** €5-15K (feasibility study only, unless contamination discovered)

## Contamination Type Analysis for CEG

### Sant'Elia (Ex Palasharp)

**Likely contaminants (speculative based on venue use):**
- Petroleum hydrocarbons (vehicle access, generators, maintenance)
- VOCs from cleaning products, paints, coatings
- Heavy metals from construction materials, equipment
- Possible asbestos (1980s construction)

**CEG capability match:** HIGH - hydrocarbons and VOCs are core bioremediation targets

### Bovisasca

**Confirmed contaminants:**
- **Arsenic** (from chemical plant waste)

**Likely additional contaminants (based on "piccola Manchester" industrial history):**
- Heavy metals (lead, chromium, mercury from chemical manufacturing)
- PAHs (polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons from industrial processes)
- Petroleum hydrocarbons
- VOCs from chemical production
- Possible dioxins/PCBs (given proximity to chemical industry cluster)

**CEG capability match:** MEDIUM
- Organic compounds (PAHs, hydrocarbons, VOCs) = CEG strength
- Arsenic/heavy metals = NOT CEG's primary capability
- **Recommendation:** Partner with heavy metal remediation specialist

### San Romanello

**Unknown** - no assessment data available

**Possible contaminants (low probability):**
- Petroleum hydrocarbons if ATM used site for vehicle maintenance
- Agricultural pesticides/herbicides (historical farm use)

**CEG capability match:** UNKNOWN until assessment

## Key Insights for Contractor Outreach

When contacting Milano contractors, emphasize:

1. **Sant'Elia is the prize:**
   - €6.2M remediation budget
   - 18,000 sqm site
   - Confirmed contamination
   - CEG's hybrid bioremediation approach ideal for mixed contaminants

2. **Bovisasca requires partnership:**
   - Arsenic contamination outside CEG's core capability
   - Does contractor have heavy metal remediation capability?
   - CEG can handle organic compounds, contractor handles arsenic
   - €1.6M budget supports multi-technology approach

3. **Timing is urgent:**
   - 6 weeks to June 26 deadline
   - Need to partner NOW to prepare bid
   - CEG can move fast on technical proposal

4. **EU Water Directive PFAS angle:**
   - New EU regulations (entered force May 11)
   - Italy must comply by December 2027
   - These sites likely have PFAS contamination (especially Sant'Elia if degreasing/cleaning chemicals used)
   - CEG's hybrid approach validated for PFAS (93% removal)
