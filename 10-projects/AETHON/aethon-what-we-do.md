# AETHON - Positioning / What We Do

## One message

AETHON is an AI-enabled Country Launch Studio. We help companies validate and launch in complex foreign markets before they hire locally.

## The simplest proposition

> Launch abroad before you hire abroad.

AETHON helps companies answer one expensive question:

> Can this country become real business for us?

We do not sell generic consulting, generic market research, or cheap outbound. We build the first commercial motion in a new country: market clarity, local buyer access, partner intelligence, first pipeline, and the path to a local team.

## Brand meaning

AETHON comes from the ancient Greek idea of something burning, blazing, or shining. For the brand, this means three things:

1. Burning desire to expand.
2. The first horse sent into the battle.
3. A signal of energy, movement, and market entry.

The brand should feel active, ambitious, and operational. AETHON is not the observer on the sidelines. It is the first mover that tests the terrain before the army commits.

## What we are

### 1. A country launch studio

We help companies go from international curiosity to an operating market-entry plan.

We build the early motion before the client hires a country manager, opens an entity, or commits a full team.

### 2. A first-mile expansion team

We own the first mile of the launch:

- Is the market attractive?
- Who are the buyers, partners, and blockers?
- What must change in the GTM?
- Can we create qualified conversations?
- Should the company hire, partner, continue testing, or stop?

### 3. A bridge between intelligence and execution

We do not stop at a report. We turn research into meetings, partner conversations, pilots, and operating decisions.

## What we are not

### 1. Not a lead-generation agency

Top of funnel is part of the work, but it is not the product.

The product is market-entry signal and activation.

Bad framing:

> We get you leads abroad.

Better framing:

> We validate whether a foreign market can become real revenue and build the first qualified pipeline.

### 2. Not a generic consultant

We are not selling advice in isolation. We are selling a structured operating system for new-country launches.

### 3. Not a Brazil-only business

Brazil is a proof point, not the category. The category is complex cross-border expansion.

Current and relevant examples:

- Klaaryo: Europe to Brazil, HR tech market activation.
- CEG: United States to Italy, environmental cleanup market entry.
- CloudWalk / [jim.com](http://jim.com): Brazil to United States, debit card issuing launch.
- Lucis Life-style opportunity: healthtech / longevity companies entering new markets through labs, partners, regulation, and local trust.

## The problem we solve

Companies often want to expand internationally, but they confuse translation with market entry.

The real questions are:

1. Who actually buys this locally?
2. What must change in our product, pricing, positioning, sales motion, or partner strategy?
3. Can we generate enough signal to justify hiring, entity setup, or local investment?

AETHON reduces the time, cost, and risk of answering these questions.

## Best-fit clients

AETHON is best for companies with all three of these traits:

### 1. They have a working business somewhere

They already have product-market fit, revenue, funding, customers, or a proven service model.

AETHON should not help companies discover product-market fit and country-market fit at the same time.

### 2. The new country is complex enough to matter

The market requires local trust, relationships, partners, procurement knowledge, regulation, integrations, or local commercial adaptation.

### 3. They are not ready to hire locally yet

They are considering expansion but do not yet want to hire a full country manager, build a local team, or commit heavy budget without evidence.

## Ideal customer profiles

### 1. Funded startups and scaleups

Especially B2B SaaS, AI, fintech, healthtech, regtech, cloud, cybersecurity, payments, industrial tech, and specialized enterprise software.

Best triggers:

- Recently raised capital.
- Investors pushing international growth.
- Competitor entering a new geography.
- Inbound demand from another country.
- Hiring for international roles.
- Website or product already partially localized.

### 2. Specialized mid-market companies

Especially companies in environmental cleanup, industrial services, healthcare services, compliance, infrastructure, and B2B services.

Best triggers:

- A large foreign opportunity exists.
- The sales motion depends on trust and partners.
- Local procurement or regulation is hard to understand.
- The company needs a market-entry operator before hiring.

### 3. VC and PE platforms

Funds that want to help portfolio companies expand internationally without building a full internal expansion function.

AETHON can scan portfolios, identify expansion-ready companies, and run validation sprints for selected companies.

## Poor-fit clients

Avoid clients with these traits:

- Pre-product-market-fit.
- No clear home-market sales motion.
- Low-ticket products where country launch economics do not work.
- Companies looking only for cheap SDR labor.
- Companies unwilling to adapt pricing, positioning, product, or process.
- Companies expecting pure commission.
- Companies that want a report but will not act on it.

AETHON should not become the outsourced cold-calling arm for companies with weak GTM.

## The three outcomes we sell

### 1. Market clarity

The client gets a practical answer to whether the market is worth pursuing.

Deliverables can include:

- Market-entry scorecard.
- ICP hypothesis.
- Buyer and partner map.
- Competitor and substitute map.
- Local risks and blockers.
- Go / no-go recommendation.

### 2. First market signal

The client gets real conversations, not just theoretical analysis.

Deliverables can include:

- Target-account list.
- Partner list.
- Localized messaging.
- Discovery calls.
- Qualified meetings.
- Pilot opportunities.
- Early revenue or pipeline.

### 3. Local operating path

The client understands what to do next.

Deliverables can include:

- Hire a country manager.
- Appoint a local partner.
- Continue testing.
- Build a local BD pod.
- Stop the market entry.
- Run a second-country test.

## Offer ladder

### 1. Expansion Readiness Diagnostic

Purpose: determine whether the company is ready to test a new country.

Typical duration: 1 to 2 weeks.

Outputs:

- Readiness score.
- Country hypothesis.
- Key risks.
- Required local resources.
- Recommended next step.

Best for: companies considering expansion but not yet committed.

### 2. Country Validation Sprint

Purpose: validate whether the target country is worth pursuing.

Typical duration: 4 to 6 weeks.

Outputs:

- Market map.
- Buyer map.
- Partner map.
- Competitor map.
- Local GTM hypothesis.
- Initial discovery conversations.
- Go / no-go recommendation.

Best for: companies with a specific country in mind.

### 3. Market Activation Sprint

Purpose: build first qualified pipeline and commercial signal.

Typical duration: 8 to 12 weeks.

Outputs:

- Target account universe.
- Decision-maker mapping.
- Localized outbound.
- Partner outreach.
- Discovery calls.
- Qualified opportunities.
- Pilot proposals.
- Weekly pipeline dashboard.

Best for: companies ready to test the market commercially.

### 4. Country Manager Install

Purpose: help the client hire, onboard, and ramp the local country manager.

Typical duration: 3 to 6 months after the first market signal.

Outputs:

- Country manager scorecard.
- Role profile.
- Interview guide.
- Candidate-source map.
- First 90-day plan.
- Pipeline handoff.
- Ongoing coaching.

Best for: companies that validated the market and want to internalize the motion.

### 5. Ongoing Expansion Board

Purpose: stay involved after the first local team is in place.

Typical duration: quarterly or monthly retainer.

Outputs:

- Pipeline review.
- Partner review.
- Market intelligence updates.
- Country manager coaching.
- Expansion decision support.

Best for: companies that want senior external support without full-time involvement.

The opportunity is broad, but the operating model must stay focused.

Do not say:

> We help anyone expand anywhere.

> We help companies with real expansion triggers enter complex foreign markets before hiring locally.

The niche is not a single country. The niche is the high-friction, high-stakes first mile of international expansion.
