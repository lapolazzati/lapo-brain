## Positioning Layers

### Short (LinkedIn headline / intro)

*I validate international expansion bets by talking to buyers before my clients spend.*

### Medium (warm intro / email signature)

*I work with founders before they commit EUR 100K+ to a new market. I run BD conversations with target buyers to test if the opportunity is real, then feed that insight back into product, pricing, and positioning. If the market holds, I open the right doors.*

### Long (deck bio / website)

*Most companies burn budget on market-entry decks and local reps before they know if anyone will buy. I flip the order: I talk to 10-15 target buyers first, extract what they actually need, and tell the founder whether the bet is worth making — or what to change before it is. If the signal is strong, I open warm doors into the market. Retainer + upside. Cross-sector. I have done this for HR SaaS in Brazil; I am doing it for bioremediation in Italy.*

---

## Why This Works

- **"Talking to your buyers"** = BD as research. Not report-writing. Signals you are in the room with decision-makers.
- **"Before you spend"** = timing. You are pre-commitment, not post-expansion.
- **"Or what needs to change"** = the actual method. For Klaaryo, this meant feedback on performance-review positioning, pricing model, and Trabalha Brasil integration.
- **No sector cage.** The motion is identical across HR SaaS and chemical remediation: find relevant buyers, extract truth, translate for the founder.

## How I Work — 4-Step Process

### Step 1: Map the Market (Week 1-2)

- Define the ICP in the target market (company size, vertical, hiring volume, pain signals)
- Build a target-account list of 30-50 companies
- Map the buyer personas (CEO, COO, VP People, Operations Director)
- Research channels: where do these buyers live? (WhatsApp, LinkedIn, industry events, referrals)
- Output: validated ICP + target-account list with contact paths

### Step 2: Run BD Conversations (Week 2-4)

- Reach out to 15-20 target buyers via warm intros, LinkedIn, or cold outreach
- Run discovery calls focused on their hiring / operational pain, not your product
- Document: what they actually need, what they currently use, price sensitivity, blockers
- Look for patterns across 5+ conversations
- Output: buyer insight report with verbatim quotes + pattern analysis

### Step 3: Feed Back & Sharpen (Week 4-5)

- Synthesize insights into actionable product / pricing / positioning changes
- Present to leadership: "The market says X, so we need to change Y"
- Examples from Klaaryo Brazil:
  - Performance reviews were the wrong hook → pivoted to WhatsApp-first screening
  - Pricing at 0.50 BRL per conversation needed stress-testing against Gupy
  - Trabalha Brasil integration surfaced as a must-have, not a nice-to-have
- Output: revised GTM strategy with evidence

### Step 4: Open Warm Doors (Ongoing)

- Convert discovery conversations into qualified pipeline
- Make warm intros to partners, distributors, or champion buyers
- Hand off qualified opportunities to the client's sales team
- Stay involved for deal coaching and follow-up strategy
- Output: 5-10 qualified meetings per market + partnership introductions

## Engagement Model

**Structure:** Monthly retainer + percentage of revenue closed in the new market
**Typical scope:** 3-6 months per market
**Ideal client:** 10-100 employees, founder-led, pre-international commitment
**I work best with:** Small companies where I have direct access to leadership

- Identifying channel partners and direct-account targets
- Regulatory-first approach: ISPRA / ARPA alignment before commercial push
- Model: equity + operational role (not pure consulting)
