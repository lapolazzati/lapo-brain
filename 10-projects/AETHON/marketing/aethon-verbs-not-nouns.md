## AETHON — Explained Using Verbs

Source: YC advice — explain your company with verbs, not nouns.
Date: 2026-06-11

---

### The noun version (what NOT to say)

> "AETHON is an AI-powered country launch studio. We help companies validate and launch in complex foreign markets before they hire locally."

Every word is a noun or noun phrase: *studio, companies, markets, launch, hire.*
The listener has to map those nouns to their own mental model. Most won't.

---

### The verb version (what to say instead)

> "We map the buyers, partners, and procurement gates in a new country. We test your pricing and pitch with real locals. We book your first qualified meetings. Then we hand the playbook to your country manager."

Every sentence starts with a verb. Every verb has an object. The listener can picture each action.

---

### The ultra-concrete version (for a founder who's never expanded)

> "You've got product-market fit in the US. Now you're curious about Brazil. We find the 10 companies there that would actually buy from you. We figure out how they buy, who decides, and what your pricing should be. We get you in the room with them. Then we help you hire someone local to take over."

No jargon. No nouns that need explaining. Just a chain of actions with a beginning, middle, and end.

---

### The venture capitalist version (still verbs, slightly more abstract)

> "We validate whether a new country can become real revenue before our clients commit headcount. We map the buyer landscape, localize the go-to-market, generate first signal, and hand off an operating playbook to a local team."

Still verb-heavy: *validate, map, localize, generate, hand off.* The nouns are simple: *country, revenue, buyers, playbook, team.*

---

### The noun-to-verb translation table

| Noun phrase (bad) | Verb phrase (good) |
|---|---|
| "We are a country launch studio" | "We map buyers, test pricing, and book meetings" |
| "We provide market-entry intelligence" | "We find who would actually buy from you there" |
| "We build first-mile expansion capability" | "We get you in the room with local decision-makers" |
| "We deliver market validation" | "We find out if the market is real before you spend $200K" |
| "We are a bridge between intelligence and execution" | "We turn research into meetings, then hand off the playbook" |
| "We offer an AI-native expansion service" | "We use AI to find buyers faster, but the output is real conversations" |

---

### The test

Read each version aloud. The noun version sounds like a website headline. The verb version sounds like something you could explain to someone at a bar.

The bar test is the right test.

---

### One-sentence version (for intros)

> "We find your first buyers in a new country, get you in the room with them, and hand off the playbook so you can hire someone local to scale it."

Verbs: find, get, hand off, hire, scale.
One proper noun, one modifier, zero jargon.
