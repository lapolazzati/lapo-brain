01 · Principles

Six rules that decide everything else

When a situation isn't covered by this manual, decide with these.

AI does the repeatable. Humans do the trust.

Mapping, research, drafts, summaries → machines. Judgment, partners, senior calls, pricing → people.

First mile, zero-to-one.

We validate motions ourselves, then hand them to someone who scales them. The founder is never the volume caller for long.

"Stop" is a deliverable.

Kill criteria are agreed before every engagement starts. Recommending a stop is a success, not a failure.

Everything compounds.

Project → intelligence → dataset → content → leads → projects. Work that can't feed the loop gets questioned.

No fake certainty.

Every claim carries source, date, confidence. Deliverables separate facts, hypotheses, and assumptions.

Be the showcase.

We sell AI-enabled market entry — our own operation must be the best demo of AI-enabled work the client has ever seen.

02 · Skill Library

One command, one repeatable job

A skill is a documented workflow triggered by a slash command — spec lives at /skills/<name>.md, runs on Claude with vault + CRM access. Build in three waves.

aethon — skill runner
/marketresearch <co> <country>

The flagship. Full country-entry pack: company profile, ICP hypothesis, competitor map, risks, signals, localized positioning, presentation skeleton.

wave 1
/brief <person>

Pre-meeting brief 30 min before every meeting: who they are, recent news, likely objections, our angle, what we want.

wave 1
/debrief <transcript>

Post-call processing: summary, objections → library, commitments, next actions, CRM draft, learnings tagged to corridor.

wave 1
/signals <corridor>

Expansion-intent scan — funding, international hiring, localized sites, partner news → ranked prospect list with intro angle.

wave 2
/accounts <client>

First 100 target accounts with decision-maker mapping and an entry angle per account.

wave 2
/partners <client>

First 30 partners — integrators, platforms, distributors, associations — with technical and commercial fit notes.

wave 2
/localize <client>

Translate the business, not the language: scripts, objection handling, and pricing rebuilt on local anchors — never converted EUR.

wave 2
/warroom <client>

Weekly client digest: pipeline movement, meetings, objections, partner status, next actions — posted by the agent after review.

wave 3
/proposal <client>

Sprint proposal from template: scope, outputs, timeline, pricing, kill criteria, explicit out-of-scope list.

wave 3
/cm <client>

Country-manager install kit: scorecard, role profile, interview guide, candidate sources, first-90-days plan, pipeline handoff.

wave 3
/atlas <engagement>

End of engagement: packages accounts, partners, objections, pricing anchors and process notes into a reusable sector-country dataset.

wave 3
The iron gate: no skill output reaches a client or prospect without human review. The skill produces the draft; the architect signs it.

Building a new skill: run it manually twice and write down every step → spec it in /skills/ → automate the repeatable parts, keep judgment steps as human checkpoints → run side-by-side with the manual version → mark active only after two runs needing <15 min of correction.

04 · CRM decision

Attio — because our business is a graph, not a funnel

Clients, partners, funds, operators and CM candidates all interconnect across corridors. Decide once, by end of month; no migration for at least a year.

Recommended

Attio

  • Flexible object model: custom objects + many-to-many links fit the client / partner / fund / signal graph
  • Auto-enriches from email & calendar — the network builds itself
  • Agent-friendly: modern API + MCP support
  • Cheap at 1–3 seats
Honest alternative

HubSpot

  • We know it deeply from client work; sequences + calling built in
  • But: funnel-shaped, expensive as seats grow, models "a fund that is also a lead source" poorly
  • If chosen for familiarity, the same data model applies

Client pipeline: Signal → Qualified → Diagnostic → Proposal → Active sprint → Install / Stop → Alumni (Expansion Board)

Partner pipeline: Identified → Contacted → In conversation → Active corridor partner  ·  every record carries a corridor tag (EU→BR, US→IT…)

05 · Engagement playbook

The Launch OS, week by week

Cadence per engagement: weekly client call + war-room digest · monthly relationship check-in (ask explicitly, log the answer) · end-of-sprint decision memo.

Hypothesis

Week 0. Why this country, why now, who buys. Write 00-brief.md with kill criteria agreed with the client.

Intelligence

Weeks 1–3. /marketresearch · /signals · /accounts · /partners. Human pass kills false positives, ranks the first 20 conversations.

Localized GTM

Weeks 2–4. /localize. Pricing rebuilt on local anchors — what does our price feel like locally — never converted EUR.

Activation

Weeks 4–12. Warm intros first. Architect takes the first 5–10 calls, then hands volume to a local operator. /brief before, /debrief after — every meeting.

Install · govern · stop

Decision gate vs. kill criteria. Go: /cm kit, CM installed with pipeline. Stop: stop memo + /atlas. Either way: a clear decision.

Exit checklist — an engagement isn't closed until:

  • Objection library updated (corridor level)
  • Partner map merged into corridor knowledge
  • Pricing anchors recorded
  • Process notes written (what we'd change)
  • /atlas run, dataset versioned
  • Reference / testimonial asked for
  • One publishable insight identified
06 · Team & talent

The best audition in the industry for country-manager careers

The talent pitch: many companies and markets in one year · an AI-native toolkit most consultancies won't have for years · a small senior team with no pyramid · and a real destination —

Join

Local BD Operator

Runs the validated motion in-country: outreach, calls, qualification, CRM hygiene. Native language, 2+ yrs BD, phone comfort, hunger for the path.

Prove

Owns a validated motion

Trained on the skills, runs the war room, escalates senior conversations. Paid local base + per-qualified-meeting bonus, slightly above market.

Destination

Country Manager — installed

The operator who runs the motion is first in line for the CM role we are paid to fill. The CM bench is tracked in the CRM — it's also a sellable asset (AETHON Pod).

Roles today

Expansion Architect (founders): client relationships, strategy, first 5–10 calls, senior conversations, go/no-go judgment — never the volume caller. AI & research layer: the skills + agents, owned by an architect until a builder joins. Specialists (legal, tax, integration) on demand — coordinated, never improvised.

Onboarding, week one

Day 1: this manual + the corridor file. Day 2: shadow the /brief → call → /debrief cycle. Day 3: run the skills on a practice account. Day 4: role-play the localized script until it sounds native. Day 5: first 10 supervised live touches.
