## Recruitment Challenges Specific to Spain

### 1. Youth Unemployment

**Current State (Feb 2026):**
- Youth unemployment (under 25): 23.8%
- EU comparison: ~10 percentage points above EU average
- Mid-20% range persistent despite overall employment growth
- Prime-age workers have significantly lower unemployment

**Root Causes:**
- Labor market segmentation
- Education system deficiencies
- Ineffective employment policies targeting youth
- Skills mismatch between education and employer needs

**Implications for Klaaryo:**
- Large pool of young job seekers requiring efficient matching
- Need for high-volume screening and engagement tools
- Opportunity to help employers reach underutilized talent pool

### 2. Skills Mismatch and Regional Disparities

- Technical, care, and skilled-trade roles difficult to fill
- Regional disparities limiting labor supply effectiveness
- Mismatch priority occupations identified by CEDEFOP
- Employers struggle with talent matching despite unemployment

### 3. Seasonal Hiring Complexity

**Tourism Sector:**
- Precise contract structures required for compliance
- Seasonal vs. permanent-seasonal contracts post-2022 reform
- High-volume hiring windows with tight timelines
- Need for quick candidate screening and onboarding

**Agriculture:**
- Chronic seasonal shortages (strawberry picking, harvesting)
- Foreign worker quotas and bilateral agreements
- Rural location challenges for talent attraction
- Short hiring windows tied to harvest cycles

### 4. Labor Market Duality

- Reforms reduced temporary contracts but duality persists
- Permanent-seasonal contracts increasing
- Complexity in contract type selection and compliance
- Need for platforms that understand Spanish labor law nuances

### 5. Employer Recruitment Pain Points

- Difficulty filling vacancies despite high unemployment
- Skills mismatch requiring better screening tools
- Cost of recruitment for high-volume, seasonal positions
- Need for multilingual candidate engagement (regional languages)
- Compliance complexity with labor laws and data protection

## Regulatory Considerations

### GDPR and Data Protection

**Employee Data Rights:**
- Right to obtain copies of all personal information held by employer
- Full access to information about data processing (purposes, categories, recipients, retention)
- Right to privacy when using digital devices
- Right to disconnect from digital tools
- Right to privacy against video surveillance in workplace

**Data Minimization in Recruitment (Spanish Supreme Court 2026):**
- Data controller obligations begin BEFORE receiving data
- Must determine what data to request, for what purpose, and by what means
- Cannot collect excessive candidate information
- Retention policies must be clearly defined and limited
- Critical for conversational AI to avoid over-collection

**Key Compliance Requirements:**
- Candidate consent for data processing (explicit, informed, freely given)
- Non-discrimination in AI screening algorithms
- Data stored within EU
- Proper data retention and deletion policies
- Transparency about automated decision-making

### Spanish Labor Law Compliance

**Estatuto de los Trabajadores (Workers' Statute):**
- Defines employment relationships and worker rights
- Contract type requirements (permanent, temporary, seasonal)
- Working time regulations and tracking obligations

**2026 Labor Reform - Working Time Tracking:**
- Radical transformation of employer obligations
- Digital working time tracking with strict technical standards
- Requirements: data immutability, integration with social security databases, real-time inspector access
- Software must have certificate of compliance with cryptographic protection standards
- Eliminates outdated recording methods

**Seasonal Work Visa Program (2026):**
- Total quota: 164,850 (88,000 for subordinate seasonal work)
- 40,000+ agricultural slots allocated
- Bilateral agreements: Morocco, Colombia, Philippines
- Employers can prioritize foreign hires in shortage sectors
- Compliance required for visa sponsorship applications

### EU AI Act

**High-Risk AI Systems:**
- Most AI for recruitment and performance evaluations classified as "high-risk"
- Requirements: strict human oversight, transparency, documentation
- Risk assessment and mitigation mandatory
- Ongoing monitoring and incident reporting
- Human-in-the-loop for final hiring decisions

**Implications for Conversational AI Recruiting:**
- Cannot fully automate hiring decisions
- Must provide explanations for AI recommendations
- Bias testing and fairness audits required
- Documentation of training data and algorithms
- Regular compliance audits

### Pay Transparency Directive

- EU directive to be transposed by June 2026
- Impacts Spanish equal pay laws
- Salary range transparency in job postings
- Pay gap reporting requirements for larger employers
- Relevant for recruitment platforms to support compliance
