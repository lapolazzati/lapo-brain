# Brazil Market Signal — AI Resume Fraud Surge

**Data point:** Robert Half Brazil survey, April 2026. 500 hiring managers across Finance/Accounting, IT, Admin, Engineering, and Legal.

---

## Key Stats

- **66%** of Brazilian hiring managers say generative AI is increasing fake or exaggerated resumes.
- **70%** of those managers have implemented *zero* countermeasures.
- **49%** report AI is increasing the volume of unqualified candidates.
- **45%** say AI facilitates the creation of false professional identities.

---

## What This Means for Klaaryo

### 1. Pain point Klaaryo can own

Klaaryo's AI recruiting via WhatsApp is already doing *conversational screening* — candidates interact with an AI agent, not just submit a PDF. This is inherently more fraud-resistant than resume-based screening.

**The narrative:** Resumes are dead. Conversations are the new credential.

### 2. Competitive differentiation vs. Talkpush / Gupy / Sólides

None of Klaaryo's competitors in Brazil are positioning around resume fraud. They all operate in the same ATS/resume paradigm. Klaaryo can claim:

> "We don't screen resumes. We verify candidates through real-time conversation."

### 3. Feature implications

Klaaryo could easily add lightweight fraud-detection signals to its existing conversational flow:

- **Consistency checks** across conversational turns (does the candidate contradict their own stated experience?)
- **Knowledge probes** tied to claimed skills ("You said you managed a team of 15. Walk me through how you handled a conflict.")
- **Cross-reference** with LinkedIn or previous application history
- **Behavioral biometrics** in WhatsApp messaging patterns

These are not new features. They are *positioning* of existing conversational capabilities against a newly urgent pain point.

### 4. Market timing

This survey dropped in **April 2026**. The problem is acute, awareness is high (66%), and resistance is low (70% have done nothing). This is a window to claim the category before competitors react.

### 5. Sales narrative for Brazil

Instead of pitching "faster hiring via WhatsApp," pitch:

> "Brazilian hiring managers are drowning in AI-fake resumes. Klaaryo replaces the resume with a verified conversation. You talk to real candidates, not polished fiction."

This reframes Klaaryo from a *speed tool* to a *trust layer*.
