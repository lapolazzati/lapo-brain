# Buy vs Build the ATS: What We Need to Know First

 *A pre-decision framework, drawn from the other side of the M&A table.*

Acknowledge the situation directly. The €5M ATS is on the table. The narrative for raising on it is real. But the decision isn't binary — it's gated on three questions whose answers you don't yet have, and answering them cheaply is a precondition for spending €5M wisely or building for 18 months wisely. Then drop the credibility line: *"At VYA, we were on the other side of this decision. The acquirer paid us a number that, in retrospect, would have been three weeks of focused build for them. They didn't know that until after the deal closed. The cost of that information asymmetry is what this memo tries to avoid."*

**The three questions (this is the body, ~250 words total):**

1. **What does Indeed Italia partnership status actually buy you globally?** **This question is answerable in two weeks with three calls.**
2. **What's the actual technical moat versus a 12-week rebuild with modern tooling?** You lived this at VYA. The thing being acquired is rarely as expensive to rebuild as the seller's price suggests, *except* for the parts that are about relationships, regulatory positioning, or accumulated edge cases. Frame the ATS as three layers: candidate acquisition (relationships — hard to rebuild), workflow logic (engineering — rebuildable in weeks), data/integrations (depends on question 1). **The €5M is justified only if layer 1 is the bulk of what you're buying.**
3. **What's the implicit integration tax on every future market?** The frontline ATS thesis works because Klaaryo can integrate with the local infrastructure layer. If Brazil's job boards are open and friendly, you don't need to buy. If they're as locked as Italy's, the buy decision in Italy is actually a buy decision *for the playbook* — and that changes the math entirely.

**Closing (3-4 sentences):** *None of these questions cost €5M to answer. The first two cost a week each. The third is what the Brazil engagement is already designed to surface. The recommendation isn't buy or build — it's: don't decide until these three are answered, and let me do the work to answer the third while the first two get pressure-tested internally.*
