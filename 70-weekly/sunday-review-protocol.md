# Sunday Review Protocol

**Purpose:** Weekly reflection & planning to align reality with goals, close open loops, and set up for focused execution.

**When:** Every Sunday evening (automated task at 18:00)

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

---

## Step 1: Time Allocation Reality Check

**Question:** Did I spend time on what actually matters?

**Process:**
1. Export last week's calendar (Monday-Friday)
2. Categorize time by project/activity:
   - Klaaryo (GTM Advisory)
   - CEG (Milano opportunity + other)
   - Catone (product + sales)
   - Internal/Admin
   - Personal/Other
3. Compare to Top 5 goals for the week
4. Ask: "What got the most time vs. what I said mattered?"

**Output:** Time allocation table + gap analysis

**Reality Check:**
> "Reality is what you did, not what you wrote on your to-do list."

The work I claimed mattered rarely matches the meetings I attended. This shows the truth.

---

## Step 2: Commitments Kept

**Question:** What did I promise and not deliver?

**Process:**
1. Scan week's Gmail inbox for:
   - "I'll send you..."
   - "Will get back to you by..."
   - "Let me intro you to..."
   - Any deadline mentioned
2. Scan WhatsApp for similar commitments
3. Check Linear for overdue tasks
4. List open loops:
   - Who's waiting on me?
   - What did I say I'd do by when?
   - What intros are pending?

**Output:** Open loops list with:
- Commitment made
- To whom
- When promised
- Current status
- Action needed to close

**Principle:**
> "Open loops create credibility risk. Close them before you close your laptop on Friday."

(But we're doing this Sunday, so close them before Monday morning.)

---

## Step 3: Lessons Learned

**Question:** What surprised me? What patterns did I miss?

**Process:**
1. Scan Granola meeting notes from the week
2. Scan Linear task comments/updates
3. Look for:
   - **Surprises:** Where did reality differ from expectations?
   - **Patterns:** What repeated that I didn't notice?
   - **Contradictions:** Where did I say one thing and do another?
   - **Insights:** What clicked this week?

**AI Prompt:**
> "Review my week's meeting notes and task updates. Where did I get surprised? Where did I miss a pattern? Where did I contradict myself?"

**Output:** 2-3 key learnings (max)

**Principle:**
> "You learned more than you realize. AI acts as your doublecheck."

Most weeks feel like nothing happened. This forces you to extract signal.

---

## Step 4: Forward Thinking — The One Decision

**Question:** What's the single most important decision for next week?

**Process:**
1. Review next week's calendar
2. Review Linear priorities
3. Review project status files (Klaaryo, CEG, Catone)
4. Ask: "What's the ONE call only I can make that shapes next week?"

**AI Prompt:**
> "Looking at next week's calendar and priorities, what's the single decision that will have the most impact? Force compress to ONE."

**Output:** One decision, clearly stated

**Not a list. One.**

**Principle:**
> "Strategy is what you don't do. The single-decision frame keeps you from optimizing everything, accomplishing nothing."

Examples:
- "Focus 100% on Milano contractor calls, deprioritize everything else until we have 1 signed partner."
- "Commit to Klaaryo Week 1 deep-dive, push Catone deploys to Week 2."
- "Book 3 Catone demos this week or kill the Q2 €1K MRR target."

---

## Step 5: Block & Tackle

**Part A: Time Block Monday**

**Process:**
1. Pull Monday's calendar
2. Add time blocks for:
   - Top priority from "One Decision"
   - Open loops to close (from Step 2)
   - Prep for Tuesday+ meetings
   - Admin/email batch (30-60min max)
3. Optimize order: Deep work first, meetings after
4. Buffer 25% for unexpected (don't pack to 100%)

**Output:** Monday schedule with time blocks assigned

**Part B: Write Monday's Open**

**Purpose:** Set tone for the week with your team (if applicable) or yourself

**Format:**
- **Context:** What happened last week (1-2 sentences)
- **Wins:** What we shipped/closed (bullets, max 3)
- **One Ask:** What I need from others this week (1 item)
- **One Problem:** What we need to solve together (1 item)

**Keep it:** No-scroll. Under 200 words. Clarity over completeness.

**Output:** Draft email/message ready to send Monday 9am
