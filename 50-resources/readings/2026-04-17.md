### **What Will Be Scarce?** (Alex Imas)
*Saved: Apr 16, 2026*

Economic analysis of post-AI scarcity: As AI automates production and makes basic goods cheap, consumer spending shifts toward services and products requiring human touch. Jobs will move toward roles where human involvement is valuable and irreplaceable by machines.

**Key framework:** When automation drops prices of category A, demand shifts to category B where humans still matter. This creates new job markets in "human-necessary" sectors (hospitality, personal services, creative judgment, emotional labor).

**Implications:** Don't train for what AI can automate - position yourself where being human is the product.

### **The Next Big Thing Will Start Out Feeling Like Nostalgia** (Andrea Gurnari)
*Saved: Apr 16, 2026*

AI agents actually began in video games (Theme Park, The Sims) where simple rules created lifelike behaviors. These early games used needs, memory, and social interaction to model artificial people - the same architectures inspiring today's AI agents.

**The pattern:** Breakthroughs often look backwards before they look forward. Games were the first true AI experiments because they had to make hundreds of entities feel alive with limited compute.

**Modern parallel:** LLM agents with memory, needs, and goals are basically The Sims with better language models. The future was already prototyped in 1989.
