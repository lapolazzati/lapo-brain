### **Building General Physical Intelligence with Sergey Levine** (Colossus / Invest Like The Best)
*Mar 31, 2026*

Podcast interview with Sergey Levine, co-founder of Physical Intelligence (raised $1B+ from Bezos, OpenAI, Thrive). Building a general-purpose brain that runs on any robot doing any task in any environment - same way one LLM can write code, summarize contracts, and answer homework questions.

**Key bet:** Generality may be more scalable than specialization. Language models beat translation-specific systems. A model trained across many robots/tasks will outperform laundry-folding or dish-washing specialists.

**The challenge:** Robotics doesn't have internet-sized datasets. You have to build them AND get robots useful enough to deploy before you can really start collecting data.

**Moravec's Paradox:** Machines find hard exactly what humans find easy. Robots beat humans at chess but struggle to pick up coffee. Last tasks robots may conquer: caring for small children (changing diapers) and caring for elderly - things we're so evolved to do we don't realize how complex they are.

**Robot Olympics:** Tested their system on everyday tasks (opening doors, washing greasy pans, picking up dog waste with plastic bag). Could handle nearly all without task-specific training.

**Timeline:** Less about technical breakthroughs, more about our relationship to the technology. How much imperfection will people tolerate from a robot in their home?
