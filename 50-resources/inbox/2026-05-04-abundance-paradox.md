# The Abundance Paradox

## Key Takeaways

• AI will likely increase total knowledge work volume rather than decrease it—following Jevons Paradox where efficiency gains expand demand
• Software engineering job postings hit multi-year highs despite AI capabilities; radiology field grew despite superior AI image recognition
• Junior roles are hollowing out (22-25 year-old software developer employment down 20% since late 2022) creating broken training pipelines
• As execution costs fall, bottlenecks shift to persuasion, prioritization, and contextual judgment—"social range becomes more valuable than cognitive range"
• 67% of AI job creation concentrates in 15 cities while displacement disperses nationally

---

## Detailed Notes

### The Core Thesis: Jevons Paradox Applied to AI

The article argues that AI won't reduce the total volume of knowledge work—it will expand it. This follows the pattern discovered by 19th-century economist William Stanley Jevons, who observed that coal consumption doubled despite more efficient steam engines.

**The mechanism**: When resources become cheaper through efficiency gains, new applications emerge faster than efficiency reduces total consumption.

> "Better productivity means more demand."

### Current Labor Market Evidence

Contrary to automation doomsday predictions:
- Software engineering job postings are at multi-year highs
- Radiology field grew despite AI having superior image recognition capabilities
- Cheaper scans increased demand for imaging services

> "The demand was always there. The price just kept it invisible."

### Why Knowledge Work is Different from Agriculture

The author distinguishes cognitive work from historical displacement cases like agriculture:

**Agriculture showed true displacement** because food consumption has natural saturation points. You can only eat so much.

**Organizational analysis has unlimited potential applications.** Most businesses currently operate with thin analysis due to cost constraints. When analysis becomes cheaper:
- Personalized attention to smaller markets becomes viable
- Comprehensive risk mapping becomes affordable
- Strategic options that were cost-prohibitive become accessible

> "Cheaper intelligence doesn't reduce human judgment needs; it reveals how much we were leaving on the table."

### The Entry-Level Crisis (Critical Problem)

**The paradox**: Job expansion coexists with broken training pipelines.

**Evidence**:
- Junior roles (where learning happens) are hollowing out
- 22-25 year-old software developer employment dropped 20% since late 2022
- Senior roles expanding while entry pathways close

**Implication**: This creates a long-term talent bottleneck even as total job volume increases. Companies want experienced practitioners but aren't creating paths to develop them.

### The Social Premium: New Bottlenecks

As AI handles execution cheaply, the bottlenecks shift upstream:

1. **Persuasion**: Getting stakeholders aligned
2. **Prioritization**: Deciding what analysis to run
3. **Contextual judgment**: Interpreting results within organizational politics

**Key insight**: "Social range becomes more valuable than cognitive range."

This means the ability to navigate organizations, build consensus, and apply judgment in messy human contexts becomes the premium skill—not raw analytical horsepower.

### Geographic Implications

**Job creation**: 67% concentrates in 15 cities (likely major tech hubs)
**Job displacement**: Disperses nationally

This exacerbates existing geographic inequality. AI benefits concentrate while costs distribute.

### Framework: Jevons Paradox Analysis

**The Pattern**:
1. Technology improves efficiency → resource becomes cheaper
2. Lower costs → demand expands into previously cost-prohibitive applications
3. Total consumption increases despite per-unit efficiency gains

**Contingencies**:
- Requires demand elasticity (unlimited potential applications)
- Requires no saturation ceiling (unlike food, housing, etc.)

**Applied to AI**:
- Execution/analysis becomes cheaper
- Organizations discover unlimited analysis opportunities that were previously too expensive
- Total knowledge work volume expands

### Questions This Raises

1. **For Catone**: Does cheaper tax due diligence expand the market (more deals analyzed) or commoditize it (lower prices, same volume)?
2. **For self-employment**: If junior pathways are closing, does this favor experienced independents or harm them by reducing client training budgets?
3. **For personal strategy**: Should you double down on "social range" skills (client relationships, prioritization, context) vs pure technical delivery?

### Connections to Existing Context

**Lapo's North Star** ("gain enough confidence and money to stay self-employed"):
- If Jevons Paradox holds, demand for experienced practitioners (like you) should increase
- But you need to position as strategic advisor (social range) not just execution (being commoditized)

**Catone strategy implications**:

- But requires educating market that analysis is now affordable for smaller deals
- Argos referral model aligns: you're in the persuasion/prioritization layer, Catone handles execution

**CEG positioning**:

## Personal Reaction / Analysis

This article reframes AI disruption from "job loss" to "job transformation + entry crisis."

The entry-level crisis is the most concerning part. If you're already experienced, the paradox works in your favor—but the industry is sawing off the ladder behind you.

For your Q2 goal (confidence + money to stay self-employed), this suggests:
- Focus on the upstream bottlenecks (persuasion, prioritization, context)
- Position yourself as strategic advisor, not just implementer
- Your experience becomes more valuable, not less

But watch the junior talent pipeline. If clients can't develop internal capabilities, they become more dependent on external advisors (good for you short-term) but the overall ecosystem gets brittle.
