## Current Signal

### Run 001 (2026-06-03)

**Strength: strong | Source trust: moderate (media, not research)**

**Key findings:**

1. **Parents want guardrails, not bans.** EdWeek survey: 8 in 10 parents want more guardrails on AI for children. Emphasis on *structured* use.

2. **UNICEF frames as literacy.** Forbes/UNICEF (Feb 2026): AI literacy is essential for parents, not just children. AI is a tool parents need to understand.

3. **AI is infrastructure for children now.** Kids and Screens (UK, 2026): "AI isn't innovation. It's infrastructure." Children experience AI as ambient and conversational.

4. **Harvard GSE: design matters.** Asst. Prof. Ying Xu (Oct 2024): "AI designed with certain principles in mind can benefit children's growth and learning, but AI literacy is essential."

5. **HN barely notices.** Single thread with 3 points. Niche interest.

### Convergence

Four independent sources (Harvard, UNICEF, EdWeek, independent blog) agree: **AI literacy for parents, guardrails not bans, infrastructure not innovation.** The consistency is the signal.

## The Tension

Kids andScreens observes: "AI systems are designed to give children *more of what they like*. Over time, that can narrow their world."

This is the builder's dilemma: engagement optimization vs. developmental breadth. You can write about this from both sides.
