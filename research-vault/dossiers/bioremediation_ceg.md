This dossier tracks: (a) whether in-situ PFAS remediation is gaining traction, (b) what competitors are doing, (c) regulatory tailwinds, and (d) how CEG's positioning holds up.

**Strength: strong | Source trust: mixed (DoD primary, PMC blocked)**

**Key findings:**

1. **DoD R&D validates dual approach.** SERDP-ESTCP (DoD research arm) runs active project ER-2715, testing ISCO + bioremediation for AFFF-contaminated military sites. Specifically: bioremediation of co-occurring chemicals as a *pre-conditioning* step for ISCO treatment. **CEG already deploys this stack.**

2. **Commercial interest is peaking.** TechConnect World hosts a dedicated PFAS Remediation symposium in 2026.

4. **Academic research blocked.** PMC article on microbial consortia for PFAS bioremediation hit reCAPTCHA. Could not verify technical claims.
