# Brazil Strategy Presentation — Klaaryo

**Date:** May 26, 2026

## Summary

Lapo presented the Brazil market entry strategy deck to the Klaaryo leadership team. The presentation covered market sizing, target ICP, competitive landscape, pricing hypotheses, and product positioning. The meeting generated strong feedback on pricing aggressiveness, ROI-calculator logic, and the importance of performance reviews as a differentiator in Brazil. Several action items emerged around Trabalha Brasil integration, target-account list building, and leveraging Tamara for local HR credibility.

---

## Key Sections Presented

### 1. Market Opportunity

- **Total companies in Brazil:** ~10M
- **Target ICP:** Companies with high frontline volume (customer support, retail, logistics, security) and 250+ employees
- **TAM estimates:**
  - Mid-market: ~€80M
  - Enterprise: ~€127M
  - **Total TAM: ~€207M**
- **Pricing hypothesis:** 0.50 BRL per conversation (~€0.05–0.10)

### 2. Brazil Labor Market Dynamics

- **Extremely high turnover** (validated by Casas Carcasas CEO):
  - Small retailers: ~92% annual turnover
  - Food & beverage: ~85%
  - Casas Carcasas: **182% annual**, 68% in first 30 days
- **Average salary:** ~€300–500/month (BRL 4,500/year)
- **Ghosting is common:** Workers simply stop showing up
- **Dismissal lawsuits are rampant:** Almost everyone who is let go files a labor claim
- **Quarterly hiring/dismissal data** is publicly available from the Brazilian Ministry of Labor

### 3. Candidate Sourcing Channels

| Channel | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| InfoJobs | Largest job board, similar volume to Indeed |
| Indeed | Second largest |
| **Trabalha Brasil** | Ministry of Labor portal; bundled with FGTS app; free for employers; huge volume in Northeast (outside São Paulo) |
| Gupy | Main competitor, launched AI recruiting recently |
| DG AI | Hyper-focused on white-collar + legal AI for labor disputes |

**Trabalha Brasil:**
- Connected to the FGTS app (almost every Brazilian employee has it)
- Proactive push notifications sent to workers when employment ends
- Free for employers
- Possible integration target (API/status TBD)

### 4. Competitive Landscape

- **Gupy** — Largest local player, most comparable
- **DG AI** — Started with legal AI for labor lawsuits, expanding to white-collar recruiting
- **Z Recruiter** — Different focus
- **Klaaryo differentiators:**
  - WhatsApp-native engagement
  - End-to-end people journey (hiring → performance → PLR)
  - Performance-review module (most competitors stop at recruiting)

### 5. Product Positioning Matrix

Lapo proposed a 2×2 matrix positioning Clario between:
- **Automation vs. CX for the candidate**
- **Tacto** (good for HR, less nice for candidate) vs. **Clario** (helps HR manage more CVs with better candidate experience)

*Feedback from Luana:* this matrix likely does **not** apply to staffing agencies, which in Italy are 85% somministrazione ( temp staffing, regulated). Need to segment by vertical before applying this lens.

## Key Decisions & Feedback

### 1. Pricing Must Be ROI-Driven and Aggressive

**Fede's feedback:**
- Pricing logic must be tightly coupled to **local labor cost**
- Klaaryo's current approach is ROI-guided: "if the client doesn't get competitive economic advantage, why would they pay?"
- **Example benchmark:** A big staffing agency in Italy closed at **€0.35/conversation** for 150K conversations/year pilot
- For high-volume clients, starting at €0.80 gets you rejected immediately
- Volumes matter: smaller volumes can sustain higher price; large volumes need aggressive entry pricing

**Action:** Lapo to build an ROI calculator that factors in Brazil labor costs, turnover savings, and hiring efficiency.

### 2. Performance Reviews Are a Major Differentiator in Brazil

**Key insight:** Performance reviews happen frequently in Brazil — often quarterly or every 6 months — even for frontline workers (retail assistants, logistics auxiliaries).
- Reviews directly determine **bonus pay** (including PLR — profit-sharing)
- Most competitors do not offer a performance-review module
- Casas Carcasas CEO said they **don't even track performance below store-manager level** in Spain; if Brazil does, there's a gap to exploit

**Fede:** *"If we can give store managers a tool to run performance reviews on all their operations staff, that's a selling point everywhere — including Italy."*

### 3. WhatsApp Dismissals = Legal Risk Mitigation

- In Brazil, dismissed workers often sue claiming "unjust termination"
- WhatsApp traceability (confirmed delivery, read receipts) is stronger evidence than paper notices
- Luana noted that messaging apps may not have full legal value vs. email/Slack (corporate systems), but it's still better than nothing
- **Opportunity:** Clario could become the system-of-record for documented, traceable dismissal communications

### 4. Multiposting / Sourcing Dynamics Still TBD

- Luana wants to understand whether Brazil follows the European multiposting model (ATS pushes to all job boards automatically)
- Historically in Italy, blue-collar hiring was 100% Indeed/Monster/Subito until ~2018; things changed fast
- **Need to map:** What is the current real sourcing mix for frontline profiles in Brazil today?

### 5. LGPD (Brazilian GDPR) — Less Strict Than EU

- Perimeter of compliance is lighter than EU GDPR
- No DPO requirement
- Still needs a mitigation strategy for enterprise adoption ( procurement will ask)
