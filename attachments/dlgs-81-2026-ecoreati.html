Environmental Criminal Law

D.Lgs. 81/2026 — Criminal Protection of the Environment

Implementation of EU Directive 2024/1203: new environmental crimes, harsher penalties, and expanded administrative liability under D.Lgs. 231/2001

📋 Legislative Decree 21 April 2026, No. 81 📋 Official Gazette No. 113, 18 May 2026 ⚠️ Effective from 2 June 2026
Relevance for CEG

The decree extends criminal protection to ecosystems, habitats, and biodiversity, tightens penalties for pollution and waste offenses, and expands administrative liability for entities. For companies operating in soil remediation and bioremediation, permit compliance, product traceability, and technical documentation are now critical.

1. New Crime: Trafficking in Polluting Products

Art. 452-bis.1 Criminal Code — Punishes anyone who unlawfully places on the market or circulates a product whose use causes significant and measurable impairment or deterioration of:

  • water or air
  • extensive or significant portions of soil or subsoil
  • ecosystems, habitats, biodiversity (including agricultural), flora or fauna
Imprisonment
2 to 6 years
Fine
€10,000 – €100,000

Aggravating Circumstances

  • Offense committed in a protected natural area or restricted zone
  • Damage to protected animal or plant species
  • Damage to an ecosystem of considerable size
  • Damage to an ecosystem with lasting effects
  • Destruction of a habitat in a protected area: increase from one-third to two-thirds

2. New Crimes on Ozone-Depleting Substances and Fluorinated Gases

Ozone-Depleting Substances (Art. 4)

Imprisonment
2 to 5 years
Fine
€10,000 – €80,000

Fluorinated Greenhouse Gases (Art. 5)

Arrest
6 months to 1 year
Fine
€10,000 – €150,000

3. Amendments to Environmental Pollution (Art. 452-bis C.P.)

  • Extended protection to the introduction of energy: heat, noise, vibrations, electromagnetic fields, electricity, light
  • Explicit inclusion of "habitats" alongside ecosystems
  • New aggravating circumstances: ecosystem of considerable size, lasting effects

4. New Definition of "Unlawfully"

Art. 452-quinquiesdecies C.P. — Conduct is unlawful if:

  1. It violates EU legislative provisions on environmental protection
  2. It violates national implementing legislative/regulatory/administrative provisions
  3. It is based on permits obtained fraudulently, through violence or threats, or by committing crimes against the public administration

Practical impact: A formally valid permit obtained through false declarations can still render conduct unlawful for criminal purposes. Procedural correctness is essential.

5. New Common Aggravating Circumstances (Art. 452-sexiesdecies C.P.)

For all environmental crimes (Title VI-bis C.P.), increased penalties if:

  • The offense yields significant profit
  • The offense is committed using false statements or documents

6. Waste: Harsher Penalties (Art. 256 D.Lgs. 152/2006)

Non-Hazardous Waste
Arrest 2–6 months
or fine €2,000–€18,000
Hazardous Waste
Imprisonment 6 months – 3 years

7. Expanded Administrative Liability (D.Lgs. 231/2001)

All new environmental offenses enter the catalog of predicate offenses under D.Lgs. 231/2001. Companies face administrative liability in addition to the criminal liability of individuals.

Required Corporate Actions

  • Update organizational models (231)
  • Strengthen environmental control safeguards
  • Verify compliance of products, permits, processes, and technical documentation

8. Common Provisions Applicable

The new crimes are subject to:

  • Active repentance (Art. 452-decies C.P.)
  • Mandatory confiscation (Art. 452-undecies C.P.)
  • Restoration of the site (Art. 452-duodecies C.P.)
  • Publication of the conviction sentence

Operational Checklist for CEG

  1. Product traceability: complete documentation of permits, safety data sheets, and EU/national regulatory compliance for every product used in remediation
  2. Permit verification: confirm all permits were obtained through correct procedures, without false declarations or omissions
  3. Waste management: remediation waste (especially hazardous) is subject to harsher criminal penalties — strengthen protocols
  4. Model 231: evaluate adoption/update of the organization and management model
  5. Technical documentation: presenting false documents is now a specific aggravating circumstance — accuracy is critical
  6. Baseline and monitoring: the decree requires "significant and measurable" impairment — environmental baseline and monitoring become defensive evidence
