# Milano Bonifica Failures: Sant'Elia, Bovisasca, and Bovisa Analysis

**Research Date:** 2026-05-14
**Purpose:** Understanding why environmental remediation (bonifica) projects failed for Milano Piano Casa sites

---

## Executive Summary

Multiple high-profile bonifica projects in Milano have failed or been abandoned due to dramatic cost overruns, ranging from 155% to 500% increases over initial estimates. The three key case studies are:

1. **Sant'Elia (Ex Palasharp)**: Olympic venue project cancelled - costs jumped from €18M to €46M (155% increase)
2. **Bovisa (MoLeCoLa/Nodo Bovisa)**: Major urban regeneration abandoned - bonifica costs reached €50M (5x initial estimates)
3. **Bovisasca**: Ongoing challenges with historical chemical contamination from the Candiani industrial legacy

---

## 1. Sant'Elia (Ex Palasharp) - Olympic Venue Failure

### Project Background
- **Site**: 18,000 m² in Lampugnano area, viale Sant'Elia
- **Original Plan**: Milano Hockey Arena for 2026 Winter Olympics
- **Developer**: TicketOne-Mca Events
- **Outcome**: Project cancelled, awarded concession revoked in January 2024

### Cost Escalation Timeline

| Phase | Cost Estimate | Source |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| Initial Bid | €18-20 million | [Il Giorno](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/nuovo-palasharp-a-rischio-la-milano-hockey-arena-per-le-olimpiadi-2026-930efd20) |
| Mid-Project (Olympic requirements) | €32-35 million | WebFetch extraction |
| Final Estimate | €40-46 million | [Urbanfile](https://blog.urbanfile.org/2024/01/28/milano-lampugnano-palasharp-troppo-costoso-riqualificarlo-ci-vuole-un-piano-b/) |
| **Total Increase** | **+155% (€28M extra)** | [Il Giorno](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/palasharp-gjvtgc90) |

### Reasons for Failure

#### 1. Olympic Requirements Added Mid-Project
The International Olympic Committee (IOC) imposed new requirements that were not in the original design:
- Two ice rinks (official matches + warm-up)
- 12 locker rooms (vs original plan)
- Three hospitality suites
- These specs were absent from initial proposal before Milano-Cortina 2026 hosting status

*Source: [Il Giorno - Olympic cost analysis](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/nuovo-palasharp-a-rischio-la-milano-hockey-arena-per-le-olimpiadi-2026-930efd20)*

#### 2. Construction Material Cost Inflation
- Pandemic-related supply chain disruptions
- Ukraine war impact on material prices
- General inflation in construction sector 2022-2024

*Source: [Il Giorno](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/palasharp-gjvtgc90)*

#### 3. Bonifica Costs (Decontamination)
While specific contamination details are not publicly documented, the site required environmental remediation that contributed to cost overruns. Current Milano Piano Casa budget allocates **€6.2 million** for potential environmental remediation of Sant'Elia.

*Source: [Cronaca Milano](https://cronacamilano.it/economia/101935-piano-straordinario-casa-milano.html)*

#### 4. Unforeseen Extra Costs Not in Bidding Process
The municipality and TicketOne could not reach agreement on responsibility for:
- Olympic-specific modifications
- "Facility downtime" expenses during post-Games construction
- Two-phase construction approach (reduced capacity during Olympics, completion after)

*Source: [Il Giorno](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/palasharp-gjvtgc90)*

### Developer Response
TicketOne-Mca Events requested:
- Public contribution of **€22.5 million** (49% of total investment)
- 50% reduction in concession fee
- Municipality rejected these terms

*Source: [Il Giorno - TicketOne request](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/palasharp-gjvtgc90)*

### Legal Outcome
- Municipality declared forfeiture of TicketOne's award (January 2024)
- TAR (Regional Administrative Court) rejected TicketOne's appeal
- Consiglio di Stato (Council of State) definitively closed case - no compensation awarded
- Area included in Piano Casa for social housing (December 2024)

*Sources: [Il Giorno - Court decision](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/palasharp-ticketone-qmdek456), [Il Giorno - TAR ruling](https://www.ilgiorno.it/milano/cronaca/palasharp-gjvtgc90)*

### Demolition Costs
Additional **€3.15 million** allocated for demolition of ex-Palasharp tensile structure.

*Source: [Il Giornale](https://www.ilgiornale.it/news/comune-fissa-affitti-375-675-euro-e-mette-3-milioni-demolire-2643922.html)*

## 2. Bovisa (Nodo Bovisa / MoLeCoLa Project) - Urban Regeneration Collapse

### Project Background
- **Site**: 90,000 m² between FN station and Via Bovisasca
- **Main Investor**: Hines (real estate development)
- **Project**: MoLeCoLa - elevated structure with green spaces, university residences, offices
- **Outcome**: Project abandoned March 2026

### Bonifica Cost Disaster

| Estimate Type | Amount | Increase Factor |
|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| Initial Estimates | ~€10 million | - |
| Final Costs | **€50 million** | **5x original** |

*Source: [Milano.zone](https://www.milano.zone/2026/03/24/milano-recesso-del-comune-hines-si-ritira-dal-nodo-bovisa-piano-molecola-affossato-tra-costi-delle-bonifiche-e-burocrazia/)*

### Historical Contamination - Candiani Chemical Plant

The Bovisa/Bovisasca area has severe legacy contamination from historical industrial activities:

**Giuseppe Candiani Chemical Industry (1862-1880s)**
- Founded 1862 for sulfuric acid production
- Transferred to Bovisa in 1880s (between Bovisasca road and railway tracks)
- Produced mineral colorants:
  - **Copper arsenite**
  - **Chrome yellow**
  - **Iron oxide**
  - Necessary for dyeing wool and silk

*Source: [Colore Digitale Blog](http://www.boscarol.com/blog/?p=8934)*

### Specific Contaminants Documented

**Heavy metals and toxic substances found in Bovisa soils:**
- **Arsenic** (from Candiani colorant production)
- **Cadmium**
- **Lead (Piombo)**
- **Hydrocarbons** (volatile and non-volatile)
- All are carcinogenic, mutagenic, and toxic in high concentrations

*Source: [Milano.zone - Bovisa contamination](https://www.milano.zone/2026/03/24/milano-recesso-del-comune-hines-si-ritira-dal-nodo-bovisa-piano-molecola-affossato-tra-costi-delle-bonifiche-e-burocrazia/)*

### Why the Project Failed

1. **Bonifica Never Completed**: The remediation of the former Montedison area in Bovisa was a necessary precondition for project launch, but was never resolved.

2. **Cost Explosion**: Unforeseen costs related to environmental remediation ballooned to approximately **€50 million**, equal to **five times initial forecasts**.

3. **Investor Withdrawal**: After months of friction with the municipal administration, Hines announced withdrawal as main investor.

4. **No Short-Term Prospects**: The cancellation left 90,000 m² without clear designation or prospects.

*Source: [Urbanfile - MoLeCoLa abandoned](https://blog.urbanfile.org/2026/03/24/milano-bovisa-molecola-abbandonato-salta-il-piano-di-riqualificazione-del-nodo-bovisa/)*

## 5. Systemic Issues with Milano Bonifica Projects

### 5.1 Scale of the Problem in Lombardia

**Regional Statistics (as of December 31, 2024):**
- **1,000 sites** currently contaminated (bonifica in progress or awaiting certification)
- **3,184 sites** already remediated
- **600+ sites** classified as "non-contaminated following Risk Analysis"

*Source: [Regione Lombardia - Siti contaminati](https://www.regione.lombardia.it/wps/portal/istituzionale/HP/DettaglioRedazionale/servizi-e-informazioni/cittadini/tutela-ambientale/bonifica-aree-contaminate/elenchi-siti+bonificati-contaminati-e-potenzialmente-contaminati/elenchi-siti+bonificati-contaminati-e-potenzialmente-contaminati)*

**Causes of Contamination:**
- Accidental events
- Spills and leaks
- Illegal dumping of waste into soil and subsoil

### 5.2 Why Cost Estimates Fail

#### A. Inadequate Initial Site Characterization

"Adequate environmental investigations, if properly sized, can **reduce costs due to unforeseen issues** and optimize remediation execution times by considerably reducing the margin of uncertainty."

The problem: Initial investigations are often inadequate, leading to discovery of worse contamination during execution.

*Source: [Tesi Online - Analisi costi bonifica Milano](https://www.tesionline.it/tesi/analisi-dei-costi-di-bonifica-dei-suoli-studio-sui-casi-della-provincia-di-milano/11658)*

#### B. Risk Analysis Shortcomings

Risk analysis (Analisi di Rischio) is supposed to determine:
- Whether a site is contaminated or not
- Toxic and carcinogenic risk levels
- Remediation target concentrations (CSR)
- Optimal remediation interventions to minimize costs

When done improperly or based on incomplete data, it leads to massive cost overruns during execution.

*Source: [Geocorsi - Analisi di rischio](https://www.geocorsi.it/N430/l-analisi-di-rischio-ambientale-strumento-predittivo-o-gestionale.html)*

#### C. Complexity of Milano Province Sites

Milano province is characterized by:
- Very high number of soil and groundwater contamination cases
- Diverse applied remediation technologies
- Wide variety of pollutant types
- Extreme variability in project costs

This makes cost estimation particularly difficult and prone to error.

*Source: [Tesi Online - Milano bonifica analysis](https://www.tesionline.it/tesi/analisi-dei-costi-di-bonifica-dei-suoli-studio-sui-casi-della-provincia-di-milano/11658)*

### 5.3 National Investment Gap

**National bonifica costs (ISPRA estimates):**
- Current market: **€3 billion per year**
- Required investment: **€30 billion total**
- Only **6% of national contaminated sites** have been remediated despite 30 years of legislation

*Source: [Recover Web - Bonifiche mercato](https://www.recoverweb.it/bonifiche-ambientali-un-mercato-da-3-miliardi-di-euro-ma-ne-servono-30-di-investimenti/)*

### 5.4 Regional Response to Systemic Issues

**Regione Lombardia initiatives:**

1. **Technical Table (September 13, 2023)**: Regional Technical Table established to manage criticalities and emergencies in bonifica procedures

2. **Cost Methodology Development**: Agreement between Regione Lombardia and Università degli Studi di Milano to:
   - Define procedures and analytical methodologies for determining bonifica costs
   - Support pricing guidelines for bonifica interventions
   - Focus on low environmental impact techniques

3. **Municipal Reporting Requirement**: Municipalities must submit annual reports (by January) on:
   - Ongoing bonifica procedures
   - Administrative, technical, and procedural criticalities encountered

*Source: [Regione Lombardia - Pianificazione bonifiche](https://www.regione.lombardia.it/wps/portal/istituzionale/HP/DettaglioRedazionale/servizi-e-informazioni/Enti-e-Operatori/ambiente-ed-energia/Bonifica-aree-contaminate/pianificazione-in-materia-di-bonifiche-dei-siti-contaminati/pianificazione-in-materia-di-bonifiche-dei-siti-contaminati)*

## 7. Current Milano Piano Casa Approach (2026)

### New Strategy - Risk Mitigation

The municipality has learned from past failures and is now:

1. **Allocating Bonifica Budgets Upfront**:
   - Sant'Elia: €6.2 million
   - Bovisasca: €1.6 million
   - Demolition (Palasharp structure): €3.15 million
   - **Total: ~€11 million**

2. **Extended Due Diligence**: Requiring comprehensive economic-financial analysis before tender award

3. **90-Year Concessions**: Longer timeframes to allow cost recovery

4. **Public-Private Partnership Model**: Shared risk between municipality and developers

*Sources: [Cronaca Milano](https://cronacamilano.it/economia/101935-piano-straordinario-casa-milano.html), [ANSA](https://www.ansa.it/lombardia/notizie/2026/04/30/dallex-palasharp-alla-bovisasca-a-milano-tre-bandi-per-il-piano-casa_3cfec046-3dbe-4830-8fb2-4559fe8e867c.html)*

### Tender Timeline
- Tenders opened: April 30, 2026
- Closing: June 26, 2026
- Three sites: Sant'Elia, Bovisasca, San Romanello

## 8. Key Takeaways - Why Bonifica Failed

### Sant'Elia (Ex Palasharp)
1. **155% cost increase** (€18M → €46M)
2. Olympic requirements added mid-project (scope creep)
3. Material cost inflation (pandemic + Ukraine war)
4. Bonifica costs underestimated
5. Developer requested 49% public subsidy - rejected
6. Legal battles ended with municipality winning, no compensation paid

### Bovisa (MoLeCoLa)
1. **500% cost increase** on bonifica alone (€10M → €50M)
2. Historical Candiani chemical plant contamination (arsenic, cadmium, lead, hydrocarbons)
3. Former Montedison area remediation never completed
4. Main investor (Hines) withdrew after months of friction
5. 90,000 m² area abandoned with no prospects

### Bovisasca
1. Same contamination legacy as Bovisa (Candiani chemical plant)
2. Known arsenic contamination
3. No documented previous remediation attempts
4. Current Piano Casa allocates €1.6M for bonifica

### Systemic Milano/Lombardia Issues
1. **Inadequate initial site characterization** - worst contamination discovered during execution
2. **Poor risk analysis** - underestimated remediation target concentrations
3. **Complex contamination profiles** - Milano province particularly difficult
4. **National investment gap** - €30B needed, only €3B/year available
5. **Low completion rate** - only 6% of Italian contaminated sites remediated in 30 years
6. **Lack of transparency** - technical reports not publicly accessible

## 9. Recommendations for Future Bonifica Projects

Based on this analysis:

1. **Invest in Comprehensive Site Characterization**:
   - Don't skimp on initial soil/groundwater analysis
   - Multiple sampling points and depths
   - Test for full spectrum of industrial contaminants

2. **Build in Contingency**:
   - Minimum 100% cost contingency for sites with industrial history
   - For chemical plant legacy sites: 200-300% contingency advised

3. **Demand Technical Transparency**:
   - Require public access to characterization plans
   - Document what was found vs what was expected
   - Share lessons learned when projects fail

4. **Longer Timelines**:
   - Don't rush bonifica for political deadlines (Olympics)
   - Adequate time for proper characterization before cost commitment

5. **Consider Site Abandonment**:
   - Some sites may be too contaminated for economical remediation
   - Institutional controls and containment may be more viable than full cleanup
