# The AI-First Marketplace

**Core thesis:** AI is a transformative platform shift like GPS-enabled smartphones. "Ten years from now, marketplaces will look very different from the way they do today." — Pete Flint

## Four Architectural Shifts

### 1. Automation Unlocked Supply

AI enables marketplaces to expand available supply in constrained sectors (therapy, driving, tutoring).

**Evolution:**
- Embedded co-pilots (assisting humans)
- → Autonomous agents (become the service provider)

**Risk:** Commoditization when training data is widely accessible

### 2. Better Demand Side Embedding

Capture the **entire customer journey**:
- Problem awareness → Research → Purchase → Repurchase

**Example:** Tailorbird (NFX-backed)
- Combines architectural visualization + procurement
- Reduces renovation planning from 1 month → 2 weeks

### 3. Reimagining the Search Box

**Shift:** Keyword-based search → Personalized guidance through workflows

**Key insight:** "Customers don't want an 'arranged marriage'" — they want to see all options, not just algorithmic picks.

**Approach:**
- Push-based recommendations (supplement, don't replace)
- Pull-based searching (still needed)

### 4. Vastly Improved Internal Efficiency

**Impact:**
- Lower demand acquisition costs
- Lower supply curation costs
- → Startups can undercut incumbents on take rates

## Three Opportunity Areas

### 1. Native AI-First Marketplaces

New platforms for:
- GPU access
- Algorithms
- **AI Agent Marketplaces** (app stores for autonomous software)

### 2. AI Leapfrogging

**Best targets:** Industries lacking digital incumbents
- Legal
- Construction
- Healthcare

**Advantage:** Bypass traditional marketplace evolution

### 3. Startup Advantages

**Attack vectors:**
- Orthogonal attacks on underserved niches
- Superior supply unlocking
- Better demand-side experiences

## AI Deployment Spectrum (5 Levels)

1. **AI Enhanced** — Baseline efficiency improvements
2. **AI Product Extension** — Adjacent opportunities
3. **AI-Enabled** — Algorithmic matching cores
4. **AI is the Product** — Complete solutions only
5. **AI-First** — Entirely new marketplaces

**Critical warning:** "AI for the sake of AI...isn't defensible."

## Catone Implications

### Where Catone Sits Today

**Level 3: AI-Enabled**
- AI classifies documents (Qwen2.5-VL)
- AI extracts structured data (Claude)
- AI powers verifiche incrociate
- But human expert reviews and decides

### Potential Evolution Path

**→ Level 4: AI is the Product**
- AI generates final DD report (not just flags)
- AI drafts risk sections with citations
- Human only approves/edits, doesn't write

**→ Level 5: AI-First (stretch)**
- AI Agent Marketplace for DD
- Commercialista uploads docs → AI agent completes full DD
- Output: Ready-to-sign report + risk memo
- Human validates signature only

### Key Decisions

**Supply unlock:**
- Today: Limited by commercialisti availability (40h manual DD)
- AI-First: Could unlock 10x supply (4h review time)
- Risk: If AI becomes commodity, differentiation erodes

**Demand embedding:**
- Today: Tool for existing DD workflow
- Future: Capture full M&A journey?
  - Pre-DD: Identify target companies
  - DD: Current product
  - Post-DD: Track tax compliance ongoing
  - Next deal: Pre-filled data from previous DD

**Search → Guidance:**
- Today: User searches documents manually
- Future: "Show me all tax risks above €50K" → AI generates ranked list with citations
- Key: Don't over-automate ("arranged marriage")

### Competitive Moat

**What's defensible:**
- Italian tax law domain knowledge (prompts, validators)
- Commercialista network (Gianni distribution)
- Data flywheel (more DDs → better anomaly detection)

**What's NOT defensible:**
- Raw LLM classification (everyone can do this)
- Document extraction (Claude API is commodity)

**Action:** Double down on domain expertise layer, not just AI wrapper.
